kare powder

For those who want to make a ready-made advanced spice mix for Japanese curry (without mixing the garam masala and curry powder separately), I've combined the ingredients into one list(added up weights for shared ingredients): 17 g coriander seeds, 16 g cumin seeds, 8 g cardamom(green), 4 g cloves, 5 g black peppercorns, 4x8cm sticks of ceylon cinnamon(equals ~5 g/2.5 tsp ground cinnamon), 0.5 g bay leaf, 1.5 g(1 1/2 star) star anise, 3 g fennel seeds, 0.7 g nutmeg, 1 g black cardamom (1 pod), 1.2 g mace (1 strand), 9 g turmeric, 2 g cloves, 7 g fenugreek seeds, 1.5 g powdered cayenne pepper, 5 g (garlic powder, 3 g paprika and 1.25 g salt. This makes 50 g. A simpler but delicious version of this is 1(2-inch) cinnamon stick, 1 bay leaf, 6 g (1 tbsp) brown mustard seeds, 5 g (1 tbsp) coriander seeds, 6 g (1 tbsp) fennel seeds, 6 g (1 tbsp) cumin seeds, 4 g (1 tsp) fenugreek seeds, 3 g (1/2 tsp) whole cloves, 2 cardamom pods, 6 g (2 tbsp) ground turmeric, 8 g (1 tbsp) ground ginger, 3 g (1 tsp) paprika, 3 g (1 tsp) cayenne. Toast spices in a pan at low heat for 1-2 minutes until fragrant and have a crunch to them, transfer the lot to a grinder or mortar and pestle and grind them into a fine powder.
