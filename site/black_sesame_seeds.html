black sesame seeds

Black sesame is the seed of a flowering plant of the genus Sesamum. It has the highest oil content of any seed, with a nutty flavor. Black sesame seeds are high in protein, iron and zinc.

Sesame seeds are added to breads, or over the top of baked goods, they are also baked into crackers or sprinkled onto a variety of foods. Popular uses of black sesame seeds also include gomashio (Japanese sesame salt) and chikki (Indian sweet brittle). The seeds need stored at 6 percent moisture or less. If the seed is too moist, it can quickly heat up and become rancid.

Weight: 1/2 cup | 2 1/2 oz | 71 g
